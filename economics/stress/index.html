02 · Why Sharpe Diverges

Bad debt vs. FX shock

Expected tail loss decomposes as P(stress) × E[slippage | stress] × notional. The slippage term is where RWA vaults separate. For D+0 NAV collateral it is two clean, stress-testable inputs. For volatile collateral it is an open-ended AMM walk.

efixDI+ (D+0 NAV redemption, slippage bounded by the FX window plus the 7.4% bonus) Volatile collateral (AMM-only exit, slippage about 1.5× the shock), illustrative
What the chart shows

The efixDI curve stays at zero until BRL falls past the bad-debt threshold. Prompt liquidation at the oracle price plus the 7.4% incentive keeps lenders whole while collateral still exceeds debt. The volatile-collateral curve leaves the floor far earlier, because AMM-exit slippage erodes recovery before the position is even nominally underwater. Same LLTV, same borrow rate, opposite tail.

05 · Methodology

Assumptions and formulas, fully reproducible

Why liquidation is an FX problem

efixDI is a 1-BRL parity claim, not a NAV-accreting share token. It is mint and burnable at par against DI fund-share entitlements held in bankruptcy-remote segregated patrimony under CVM Resolution 88/2022. CDI yield reaches holders as monthly BRL cash distributions, never as token appreciation. The token's BRL price stays at 1, so its USD collateral value moves only with BRL/USD.

collateralUSD = quantity × 1 BRL × FXBRL/USD

No fund-NAV oracle sits in the liquidation path. Pricing is Chainlink BRL/USD, the same feed Morpho already uses for BRZ, BRLA, and other BRL-stable collateral.

Health factor and the three thresholds

LTV0 = (L − 1) / L  ·  HF = LLTV / LTV  ·  after BRL drop d:   HF(d) = LLTV · (1 − d) / LTV0

FX drop to auto-deleverage (HF 1.15):   ddlv = 1 − LTV0 / (LLTV / 1.15)
FX drop to liquidation (HF 1.00):      dliq = 1 − LTV0 / LLTV
FX drop to bad debt (collateral = debt):   dbd = 1 − LTV0

Liquidation incentive (Morpho Blue)

LIF = min( 1.15 , 1 / ( 1 − 0.3 · (1 − LLTV) ) )  →  at LLTV 77%, LIF = 1.0741 (7.41% bonus)

At the liquidation trigger the position holds 30% over-collateralisation against debt (1 / 0.77). A liquidator repays debt D, seizes D × LIF of collateral at the oracle price, then exits through NAV redemption over the settlement window.

Liquidator participation and lender bad debt

Liquidator clears if:   (FX window move w) + (op cost c) ≤ LIF − 1 = 6.9%

Lender bad debt per $1 of debt = max( 0 , 1 − (1 − d) / LTV0 ) , non-zero only once d > dbd

Two independent things must both fail for lenders to lose. First, the intraday window move exceeds the 6.9% bonus, so liquidators step back. Second, BRL gaps past the bad-debt threshold faster than the position can be cleared. For BRL, a liquid G20 currency with circuit breakers, a 3-hour move beyond 6.9% is a 10-sigma event under normal volatility, and a sub-3-hour move past the bad-debt threshold implies a sovereign-grade dislocation with capital controls.

The binding operational parameter, for curator calibration

The real constraint is not AMM depth. On-chain efixDI/USDC depth is thin today and serves only the residual fraction. The live liquidation path is NAV redemption through EFIX's regulated infrastructure, whose throughput is finite. The safe supply cap therefore comes out of the redemption-pipe throughput over the liquidation horizon, plus instant AMM-residual depth. This is the parameter we want the curator's risk team to own and size, next to the IRM curve and the deleverage trigger.

Two scope honesties

1. Audit scope. OpenZeppelin audited the efixDI token (15/15 resolved, including one high-severity finding fixed with a custody redesign). The collateral vault as a whole and the off-chain backing sat outside that scope. A dedicated vault audit is being scoped to the curator's go-live, and the off-chain backing is covered by the monthly Harris & Trotter supply-vs-NAV attestation.

2. Oracle. The May 2026 staleness incident came from a threshold set inside the 24-hour heartbeat. The fix has three parts: a staleness threshold above 25 hours, an external uptime monitor with hourly checks, and webhooks on low operator-wallet gas and on RPC failure.
